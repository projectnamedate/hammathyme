## 1. AI Producer hiring landscape — May 2026 refresh

The "AI Producer" title has solidified into a real role since Q1 2026, but it's still fragmented across three archetypes: (a) **AI Creative Producer** at agencies (Media.Monks, Stink, R/GA), (b) **AI Video Producer** at AI-native studios (Runway Studios, Pika), and (c) **Senior Creative Designer & AI Image/Video Producer** hybrids that show up on freelance/remote boards.

**What's shifted since April:**
- **Toolchain fluency is now itemized, not vibes-based.** Listings explicitly name Runway Gen-4.5, Veo 3.1, Kling 3.0, Pika, Luma, Seedance 2.0 — and demand candidates "chain 3–5 AI tools in a single production" ([Jobgether listing, Apr 2026](https://jobgether.com/offer/69e51135c646310ee39454d9-senior-creative-designer-ai-image-video-producer); [Remotive 55labs listing](https://remotive.com/remote/jobs/design/creative-ai-video-producer-2765954)).
- **Portfolio expectations are codified.** Multiple listings now require "3–5 recent projects: at least 1 video ad, 1 design, 1 AI-generated piece" with reels under 90 seconds. A maintained **prompt library** is being explicitly requested as a deliverable.
- **Hybrid craft language.** Roles want producers fluent in "rule of thirds, color theory, AND latent diffusion / temporal stability" — i.e., cinematic vocabulary plus model behavior ([ProductionHub, 2026](https://www.productionhub.com/blog/post/will-ai-replace-film-video-production-jobs-in-2026-inside-the-real-industry-shift)).
- **Experience floor moved up.** Senior roles now ask 7+ years traditional production + 1+ year dedicated AI work. A year ago the AI-side ask was "exposure."
- **Runway Studios is publicly hiring screenwriters, animators, creative producers, business affairs** ([Runway careers](https://runwayml.com/careers)) — confirms AI-native shops now staff like real production companies.

**Salary bands quoted:**
- Runway-tier AI roles: **$93k–$225k** ([ZipRecruiter Apr 2026](https://www.ziprecruiter.com/Jobs/Runway-Ai)).
- Pika-tier: **$100k–$400k** range (heavily skewed to engineering — producer roles cluster at the lower end).
- "AI Video" generalist roles: **$57k–$172k** ([ZipRecruiter](https://www.ziprecruiter.com/Jobs/Ai-Video)).
- Note: Amazon "AI Producer" as a discrete title isn't surfacing — Amazon AI roles are still ML-engineer-coded ($176k–$399k ML; $130k–$220k generalist AI). The spec's claim that Amazon hires "AI Producers" should be softened to "AI-adjacent creative roles."

**What hiring managers want, May 2026 — bullet list:**
- Multi-tool chaining (Runway + Veo + Kling + Suno + ElevenLabs in one piece)
- Prompt library and reusable workflow documentation
- Demonstrable temporal/character consistency across shots
- Cinematic vocabulary alongside model fluency
- Speed: prompt-to-deliverable in hours, not days
- A reel that proves judgement, not just access

## 2. AI video / image / audio model landscape — May 2026

State of the art has compressed into roughly six video models, three image models, three audio models. Here's what's current, what's name-droppable, and what's dead.

**Video — current (worth name-checking):**
- **Veo 3.1** (Google DeepMind) — cinematic realism, native synced audio, 1080p/24fps. The "default cinematic" choice ([AI/ML API blog](https://aimlapi.com/blog/google-veo-3-1)).
- **Runway Gen-4.5** — currently #1 on Video Arena. "Hyper-Realism Mode" + 120fps output via latent diffusion transformer ([AI Tool Analysis](https://aitoolanalysis.com/runway-review/)).
- **Kling 3.0** (Feb 2026) — multi-shot sequences (3–15s) with subject consistency across angles. The character-consistency winner.
- **Seedance 2.0** (ByteDance, Feb 2026) — first unified audio-video joint generation, phoneme-level lip-sync in 8+ languages ([Lushbinary comparison](https://lushbinary.com/blog/ai-video-generation-sora-veo-kling-seedance-comparison/)).
- **Luma / Pika** — still alive, more niche. Luma is the "ray-traced" pick; Pika the prosumer fast-iteration tool.
- **Hedra** — character/avatar specialist; widely used for talking-head AI characters.

**Sora 2 — flag for the spec:** The spec says Sora API sunsets September 2026. **Confirmed**, but the consumer-facing **Sora web/app discontinued April 26, 2026** — only the API remains until **Sept 24, 2026**. So as of today, Sora 2 is effectively a B2B-only model with a hard expiry. Mention with caveat or skip entirely.

**Image — current:**
- **Nano Banana 2** — leads on skin texture / micro-pore detail / subsurface scattering ([Starkie comparison](https://starkie.ai/articles/nano-banana-2-vs-flux-pro-vs-midjourney-v7-realistic-ai-portraits)).
- **FLUX.2 Pro** — recently released, the "open-ish" prosumer pick ([Medium review](https://medium.com/@leucopsis/flux-2-pro-review-and-comparison-with-midjourney-v7-and-with-nano-banana-pro-337224a5551f)).
- **Midjourney v7** — aesthetic-poll winner, especially eyes/iris/catchlights. Still the "art-director default."

**Audio — current:**
- **Suno v4.5** (early 2026 release) — consumer leader for music ([AI Magicx 2026](https://www.aimagicx.com/blog/suno-vs-udio-vs-elevenlabs-music-comparison-2026)).
- **Udio** — musician-favored quality.
- **ElevenLabs Music** — license-clean, enterprise-safe, trained on licensed data only (key differentiator for brand work).
- **ElevenLabs (voice)** — still the voice-clone default.

**Dead / deprecated / DON'T name-drop:**
- Sora 1, Runway Gen-3, Pika 1.x, Kling 1.x, Stable Video Diffusion (1.x), Gen-2 — all retired/superseded.
- Older Midjourney versions (≤v6) — still functional but considered passé.
- Sora 2 in any "live tool we'll use forever" framing — sunset is locked in.

## 4. "AI Producer" SEO competitive landscape

The SERP for **"AI Producer"** is dominated by:
- Job-board pages (ZipRecruiter, Indeed, Upwork "hire" pages)
- Definitional/explainer content from SEO-bait blogs
- A few agency landing pages from Curious Refuge and similar AI-film schools
- Almost zero individual portfolios

**Verdict: the field is wide open for a portfolio to rank.** No dominant personal brand owns "AI Producer" as a personal SEO target. Compare to "AI Engineer" or "Creative Director" where Top 10 is locked.

**Adjacent terms checked:**
- **"AI Creative Producer"** — same pattern, dominated by Curious Refuge job board ([curiousrefuge.com/ai-jobs-board](https://curiousrefuge.com/ai-jobs-board)) and Upwork hire pages. Wide open.
- **"AI Video Producer"** — slightly more contested by tool-comparison content (Lava Media's "Top 10 AI Video Production Agencies in 2026") but still no dominant individual portfolio.
- **"Hire AI Producer"** — Upwork/Contra/BotPool dominate. A portfolio with strong on-page schema + a `/hire` route can plausibly crack page 1.

**Content gaps Hammer's `/notes` can win:**
1. **"What an AI Producer actually does (May 2026)"** — first-person, role-defining. Hits the definitional gap and Information Gain signal Google's [March 2026 Core Update](https://www.evertune.ai/resources/insights-on-ai/googles-march-2026-core-update-a-content-best-practices-guide-for-seo-and-ai-search) explicitly favors.
2. **"How to chain Veo 3.1 + Kling 3.0 + Suno v4.5 in one shot"** — workflow content, name-drops models that have search volume right now.
3. **"Hiring an AI Producer: a checklist"** — captures "Hire AI Producer" intent.
4. **"What hiring managers want from an AI Producer reel in 2026"** — owns the recruiter/hiring-manager search vector.
5. **Case studies with proprietary process + before/after** — Information Gain compliant; AI search engines (ChatGPT search, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) cite this kind of original-data content disproportionately ([Search Engine Land 2026 predictions](https://searchengineland.com/ai-search-visibility-seo-predictions-2026-468042)).

**SEO note for spec:** The spec's general "publish notes" plan is correct; it should be sharpened to **lead with definitional + workflow content** because the SERP is uncontested on those terms specifically.

## 5. Editorial / cinematic typography trends — May 2026

**Display serifs in active rotation on premium agency / director sites:**

- **PP Editorial New** (Pangram Pangram) — still the dominant editorial serif in 2026 on agency/director sites. Free-to-try license; commercial license required for production. Source: [Pangram Pangram](https://pangrampangram.com/products/editorial-new).
- **GT Sectra** (Grilli Type) — broad-nib calligraphy meets surgical precision. Paid only. Strong for headlines with attitude.
- **Tiempos Headline / Tiempos Fine** (Klim) — economy-and-legibility editorial reference. Klim license (paid).
- **Söhne** (Klim) — still the dominant body sans on Awwwards-tier dark sites. The "Apple-adjacent" default for quiet luxury.
- **Inter Tight** — open-source workhorse. Free. Ubiquitous on dark portfolios where budget matters.

**Newer entrants worth considering (May 2026):**
- **PP Fragment** (Pangram Pangram, 35th release) — oscillates between high-contrast serif, mid-serif "Glare," and a strong sans across **32 weights**. Born from 19th-century lettering. Strong fit for a single-foundry-system approach where one family carries the entire brand voice ([PP Fragment](https://pp-fragment.com/)).
- Pangram Pangram, Klim, Grilli Type, Commercial Type, ABC Dinamo, Dinamo, and Lineto remain the foundries shaping 2026 ([Creative Boom 50 fonts](https://www.creativeboom.com/resources/top-50-fonts-in-2026/)).

**Dominant body sans on Awwwards-tier dark sites in 2026:**
1. **Söhne** (most frequent on premium sites)
2. **PP Neue Montreal** (Pangram, free-to-try) — heavy use on indie agency sites
3. **Inter Tight** (open-source)
4. **ABC Diatype** / **ABC Monument Grotesk** — for the more architectural/Swiss feel

**Spec callout:** PP Editorial New + Söhne + a mono accent (e.g., **PP Neue Corp Mono** or **JetBrains Mono**) is the "safe-but-correct" 2026 stack for a director/AI-Producer site. If the brand wants to be more distinctive, swap PP Editorial New for **PP Fragment** or **GT Sectra Display**.

## 6. Autonomous AI character / agent showcase precedents

**Verdict: the space is very unoccupied for portfolio framing.** This is genuinely a defensible differentiator for Hammer.

**What exists:**
- **Tools that run AI agents on socials** (Hypefury, Typefully, NoimosAI, TwitterAIAgent, Sintra Soshie) — these are SaaS products, not portfolio-coded experiences ([NoimosAI](https://noimosai.com/en/blog/top-5-ai-agents-for-x-twitter-in-2026-revolutionizing-your-social-strategy); [OpenTweet](https://opentweet.io/blog/ai-agents-that-post-to-twitter-2026)).
- **Autonomous AI character experiments** — academic/technical writeups (e.g., [MDPI study on 7-layer character architectures](https://www.mdpi.com/2079-9292/14/21/4161) deploying agents on Twitter/Discord/Telegram for 18 days; [Upstash Hacker News X Agent](https://upstash.com/blog/hacker-news-x-agent)) — but these are research artifacts, not portfolio sites.
- **AI-only social network experiments** like Agent4Science / Moltbook — agent-to-agent platforms, not human-facing portfolio showcases.
- **Truth Terminal-era character agents from 2024–2025** — those sites are mostly dead or dormant; very few survived into 2026 with active live displays.

**What does NOT exist (verified absence):**
- A producer/director portfolio that **frames a live autonomous character as the headline case study**.
- A polished site embedding **live agent output (latest post, response cadence, follower delta)** as a hero element.
- A "I built and run autonomous AI characters end-to-end" personal-brand positioning.

**Conventions worth borrowing if Hammer is first:**
- **Live tickers** (latest post timestamp, cumulative posts, follower count) embedded as a permanent header strip
- **A "console" aesthetic** — terminal-coded panels showing the agent's last N actions
- **Embedded X/Telegram post previews** with live timestamps, refreshed via lightweight cron + ISR
- **"Watch it live" CTA** on the agent's case-study route — invites scrutiny rather than hiding the receipts

**Spec callout — confirmed:** Hermes-style autonomous-character framing is genuinely whitespace. The spec's claim that this is Jeff's differentiator holds. Recommend leaning into it harder: make it a **route**, not a paragraph. The site's `/work/agents` category page with live data > "I also do autonomous agents" in an About blurb.

## What this means for Hammer's brand guide

1. **Lead with the autonomous-agent differentiator.** It is genuinely unoccupied as portfolio territory. The `/work/agents` category page should host live post data, agent metrics, and a "watch it run" CTA per character. Everything else is a tool-fluency claim that competitors can copy; this one isn't.

2. **Name-drop the May 2026 stack precisely, and only the May 2026 stack.** Veo 3.1, Runway Gen-4.5, Kling 3.0, Seedance 2.0, Suno v4.5, ElevenLabs Music, Nano Banana 2, FLUX.2 Pro, Midjourney v7. Drop Sora references entirely or caveat them as "API-only until Sept 2026." Drop Gen-3, Sora 1, Pika 1.x, Kling 1.x.

3. **Type system: PP Editorial New + Söhne + mono accent is the safe choice; PP Fragment is the distinctive one.** Body sans should be Söhne, PP Neue Montreal, or Inter Tight — pick one and stay there. Avoid mixing more than three families.

4. **SEO is carried by case-study copy, not a blog.** Jeff dropped /notes 2026-05-04 — visible writing reads as anti-signal in 2026 visual circles. The portfolio's case-study approach blocks (model name, cost, time, output spec per piece) carry the SEO weight instead. Personal-portfolio SERP for "AI Producer" is still wide open; the win is original workflow data inside case studies, not a /notes page.

5. **Visual reference set: ManvsMachine + Silent House (SOTD Apr 28 2026) + Voku.Studio (SOTD Feb 21 2026) + Stink Studios brand case.** Borrow specifics, not vibes — slow type masks, hover-driven color grading, lowercase mono nav, project tiles that desaturate on idle. Daniel Wolfe / Anorak remain valid director-coded references.
