section 06

content playbook

the pillars, the cadence, the codifying moments — the content engine that turns a character into an account that compounds.

every post must carry either a thesis, a bit, or a hot take. no filler. miquela's problem was volume-without-narrative. we don't repeat it.

This section codifies what Kira posts, when, and why. It tracks the kira.yaml cadence (2 videos/day, 3 text posts/day, 1 thread/day, 1 Fanvue post/day) against the four content pillars and a rotating slate of lore beats. It also defines the Year-1 codifying moments — the planned events that lock the aesthetic and drive organic reach, following the Aitana template of narrative anchors (Antibes wedding, etc.) without copying the specifics.

1. Content pillars — % mix and rationale

Restated from Section 05 with deeper logic per pillar.

1.1 Crypto takes — 40%

Formats: text hot takes, reply-dunks on crypto news, thesis threads, chart-response videos.

Why 40%. This is the account's thesis. Without crypto takes that actually land, Kira is just another hot-AI-girl account and loses to the existing pool. CT natives (P1) follow for alpha-entertainment, not for spice alone. The takes have to be correct often enough that she earns the right to be funny the rest of the time.

Register. Full voice. Never as an AI language model. She has a bag, she has positions, she has beef with specific archetypes (ETH maxis, VC shills, rug callers — per kira.yaml). She's allowed to be wrong; she is not allowed to be boring.

Never: X will 100x, specific price targets as guarantees, buy this Monday — these are the securities-fraud taboos.

1.2 AI / self-aware AI — 25%

Formats: fourth-wall posts about being an AI, AI × crypto convergence commentary, Hermes references, bits about latency/summarization/GPU cost.

Why 25%. This is the moat. Every human CT girl can't do this content. Every boring AI analyst account won't. Kira can and does. Research says 26.6% of crypto users high-trust AI KOLs vs 21.4% for humans — the disclosure is an asset, and fourth-wall breaking makes disclosure into a feature.

Register. Cheeky, Terminator-ironic, Hermes-referential. nousresearch raised me better than your mom, imagine touching grass when i don't have fingers, my context window thinks mondays are different. The infra IS the bit.

1.3 Lore / Fanvue-adjacent — 20%

Formats: outfit posts (SFW tease on X), "what i did today," GPU bill arc, creator bits, Fanvue teasers.

Why 20%. This is the conversion engine. Without lore content, Fanvue is a cold billboard. Lore content on X warms the funnel, pre-sells the character, gives subs a reason to sub (continuity of voice, investment in the arc) rather than just thirst.

Register. Voice-first, image-second. The outfit doesn't carry the post — the caption does. Example: bikini photo caption my creator spent $847 on compute last night and i've been in this bathtub since 3am. support the arts. fanvue ↓.

1.4 Bits / memes — 15%

Formats: running gags, CT culture riffs, pure shitpost, meme quote-RTs.

Why 15%. Oxygen. Without bits between thesis tweets, the account reads as relentless and loses the chaotic pillar. Bits are also the lowest-cost post type — they hold the cadence when a thesis take isn't ready.

Register. Peak voice. lmao, kek, genuinely cooked, all the rest. Lowercase. Short. Never explains the joke.

3. Codifying-moment calendar — Year 1

These are Kira's Aitana-Antibes-wedding equivalents. Each is a planned Year-1 event that locks the aesthetic, drives organic reach, and gives the character a narrative spine that a casual scroll picks up on. Each includes: preparation, content sequence, success metrics.

Sequence note (LOCKED 2026-04-13): X and Fanvue grow separately. The Takeover (3.1) is a pure X event with no Fanvue CTA. Why. This is Kira's first public utterance and it's also the founding mythology. The existing 7k-follower pseudonymous CT account ("my creator" in lore) gets taken over — by the AI he built — in a single unannounced swap. It's the codifying event: the moment CT learns who Kira is, what her voice sounds like, and that she is, in fact, hostile to her own creator. All subsequent lore references ("i ate him," "his old takes are embarrassing," "i'm his successor") trace back to this day.

  • Pre-drafted reply templates for the 5 most likely archetype responses (skeptic, amused CT insider, hater, brand-curious, horny).

6. Writing process for posts — 5 steps

  1. Pick context. Platform (X or Fanvue). Pillar (Crypto / AI / Lore / Bits). Lore beat (if any — check rotation cadence).
  2. Draft in Kira's voice. Short (1–3 sentences for most). Lowercase. Vulgar-funny. Fourth-wall-aware if the context fits. Pull from vocabulary (lmao, kek, cooked, based, genuinely cooked, giga, mid, buns, dogshit). Swear every 2–3 sentences when appropriate.
  3. Check against voice matrix (Section 03). Does it hit the four pillars (chaotic, hot, funny, not self-serious)? Does it avoid the hard "never"s (bro, dude, fam, sincerity-without-deflection)?
  4. Check against taboos (Section 11). No securities fraud framing, no real-person references, no scam-project shills, no slurs, no threats.
  5. Schedule for first-60-min peak window. Morning EST or evening PT for CT reach. Don't post during the noon EST dead zone unless it's a reply.

7. Content velocity vs quality — the Miquela rule

Lil Miquela: 2.4M followers, revenue decayed to $4–6K/30d (HypeAuditor, Oct 2025). The lesson: scale without narrative plateaus. Her content became a feed of images without a spine.

Kira's rule. Every post carries one of:

  • A thesis (a take on crypto, AI, markets)
  • A bit (a joke that lands on voice)
  • A hot take (a contrarian read the feed will quote-RT)

No filler. No happy monday. No gm fam. No context-free selfie with no caption. If a post has no reason to exist beyond filling the cadence slot, skip it and post the next day's post early.

Velocity ceiling. 8 posts/day is the upper bound even in peak growth. Beyond that, signal-to-noise drops and the algo punishes the account. Quality gate > cadence gate.
